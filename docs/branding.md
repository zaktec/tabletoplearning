# TableTopLearning Branding Guide

## Brand name

Always write the brand name as **TableTopLearning**.

Do not use:

- Tabletoplearning
- TabletopLearning
- Table Top Learning

## Brand summary

TableTopLearning provides structured online courses with real tutor support. The brand should feel warm and encouraging while remaining professional, practical and trustworthy for learners and parents.

## Brand personality

- Friendly, not childish
- Encouraging, not exaggerated
- Professional, not formal or distant
- Practical and easy to understand
- Calm, modern and dependable

## Colour system

The website uses the following shared CSS tokens:

```css
:root {
  --color-primary: #bd5500;
  --color-primary-hover: #963f00;
  --color-background: #fff8ec;
  --color-surface: #ffffff;
  --color-surface-soft: #fff3df;
  --color-heading: #241a14;
  --color-text: #594a40;
  --color-text-muted: #78695e;
  --color-border: #e8d9c7;
  --color-focus: #7a3d00;
}
```

The primary orange is darker than the original bright orange so white button text has accessible contrast.

### Colour usage

- Use light cream for the page canvas.
- Use white for the header, footer and most content cards.
- Use orange for primary buttons, important active states and small accents.
- Reserve orange borders for selected, recommended, hover or keyboard-focus states.
- Use pale orange sparingly for secondary buttons, badges and numbered indicators.
- Use charcoal for headings and brown-grey for supporting text.
- Use neutral borders by default.
- Do not introduce unrelated accent colours.

## Typography

- Preferred heading stack: Poppins, Inter, Segoe UI, sans-serif
- Preferred body stack: Inter, Segoe UI, Roboto, Arial, sans-serif
- Headings should be clear, compact and moderately bold.
- Body copy should generally be 15-16px with a line-height between 1.45 and 1.65.
- Supporting card and pricing text should be at least 14px.
- Uppercase labels may use 10-12px text with increased letter spacing.
- Avoid very long line lengths on desktop.

## Logo and wordmark

- Use the `TTL` icon with the **TableTopLearning** wordmark.
- Keep the icon legible and visually secondary to the full brand name.
- Place the logo on the left side of the header.
- Increase the icon slightly on mobile while keeping the header compact.
- Do not distort, recolour or separate the wordmark into multiple words.
- Avoid additional symbols or decorative logo treatments.

## Interface style

- Prefer simple white surfaces against the cream page background.
- Use rounded corners consistently, without making every item a prominent card.
- Keep shadows subtle and uncommon.
- Use one decorative shape at most in a major section.
- Keep primary actions orange and secondary actions pale or neutral.
- Maintain comfortable touch targets and visible keyboard focus.
- Use whitespace to separate content rather than repeated borders or background colours.

## Photography and illustration

When imagery is introduced, it should feel natural, inclusive and focused on real learning. Avoid generic corporate imagery, exaggerated success claims and decorative visuals that compete with course information.

## Voice and messaging

Use clear, concise and learner-focused language.

- Prefer **learner** over **student**, unless quoting a real testimonial.
- Explain the next step directly.
- Focus on structure, steady progress, tutor support and confidence.
- Avoid inflated promises, invented outcomes and education jargon.
- Keep headings short and supporting copy easy to scan.

Examples:

- Structured online courses with real tutor support.
- Learn week by week.
- Choose the support level that suits you.
- Small steps, steady progress and better confidence.

## Accessibility

- Preserve sufficient colour contrast for text and controls.
- Never rely on colour alone to communicate selection or status.
- Use semantic links and buttons with visible focus states.
- Maintain readable type sizes and comfortable touch targets.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid unnecessary animation.
